# QuestLearn 4–6 Minute Pitch — Take Sheet (Finn-style)

Target runtime: **~5:00**
Deck source: `https://questlearn-nu.vercel.app/pitch/v2-social-engagement`

## Recording model
- 1 take = 1 slide focus + 1 narration block
- Keep each take independent and named sequentially
- Replace only failed takes, then re-concat final cut

## Takes

### T01 (0:00–0:28) — Title / Hook (Slide 1)
**Voice:**
"QuestLearn turns student engagement into active learning. We built it for Years eight to ten, aligned to Australian Curriculum version nine, so students actively think, not just passively consume content."

### T02 (0:28–0:58) — Problem Framing (Slide 2)
**Voice:**
"Today, student attention is high online but often low in traditional instruction. The gap is not motivation alone, it is learning design. Students need formats that pull them into explanation, retrieval, and reasoning."

### T03 (0:58–1:28) — AU Evidence Context (Slide 3)
**Voice:**
"Our framing is Australia-first. We use current youth digital behavior context to justify why active, format-flexible learning matters now, and why engagement should be redirected into deliberate practice."

### T04 (1:28–1:58) — Attention Shift to Learning Behavior (Slide 4)
**Voice:**
"The key shift is from content delivery to learning behavior. QuestLearn guides students to respond, compare, explain, and refine. That is how curiosity becomes durable understanding."

### T05 (1:58–2:30) — Learning Pattern + Socratic Loop (Slide 5)
**Voice:**
"At the core is a Socratic loop. The AI does not just give answers, it asks better questions. Students articulate thinking, receive prompts, and iterate. This creates active recall and metacognitive reflection."

### T06 (2:30–3:05) — Mini Apps Walkthrough (Slide 6)
**Voice:**
"Students can learn through meme, flashcards, concept map, and debate modes. Each mode targets a different cognitive pathway, from memory to structure to argumentation, while staying tied to the same topic goal."

### T07 (3:05–3:37) — Teacher Action Loop (Slide 7)
**Voice:**
"Teachers get visibility into engagement and can act quickly. They can spot weak areas, assign targeted quests, and close the loop between student activity and classroom intervention."

### T08 (3:37–4:09) — AI on Both Sides (Slide 8)
**Voice:**
"AI supports both sides of the learning equation, student growth and teacher decision-making. Students get adaptive prompts, and teachers get practical signal instead of noisy dashboards."

### T09 (4:09–4:41) — Today + Next (Slide 9/10)
**Voice:**
"Today, QuestLearn delivers production-ready active learning flows. Next, we extend into collaborative study pathways and deeper teacher orchestration, while preserving student agency and psychological safety."

### T10 (4:41–5:05) — Close / Submission Fit (Slide 11)
**Voice:**
"QuestLearn is built for AI-incorporated active learning, combining engagement, curriculum alignment, and measurable teaching feedback. Thank you, we are ready for demo day."
