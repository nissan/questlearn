## Full narration (clean read)

In remote New South Wales, Aboriginal students fall nearly three years behind their city peers by Year Nine. Not because they're less capable — because consistent, quality teaching is hard to reach. Teacher turnover in remote schools runs as high as sixty percent a year.

A Year Nine student in Bourke opens QuestLearn. She's stuck on photosynthesis. She doesn't need another worksheet. She picks a meme.

The AI generates a curriculum-aligned meme — mapped to Australian Curriculum v9. Then it asks her a question. Not to test her. To make her think.

She answers wrong. There's no red cross. No score dropped. Just encouragement — and another question. The AI never gives the answer. It builds understanding, one question at a time.

Five formats: Game, Story, Meme, Puzzle, Short Film. The student always chooses. Never assigned. Every format maps to a different way of learning.

The teacher sees which topics the class is struggling with — in real time. A student in Bourke gets the same AI tutor as a student in Bondi. QuestLearn.

## Notes for Finn

**Pacing guidance:**
- Scene 1 (0:00–0:15): 15 seconds of near-silence. Hold the darkness or landscape. Don't rush to show UI — the weight needs to land.
- Scene 2 (0:15–0:27): First UI appears here. Keep transitions crisp but not flashy. Dissolve, not wipe.
- Scene 3 (0:27–0:44): The meme card is the visual centrepiece of this variant. Give it 3–4 seconds of full-screen hold before the Socratic question appears.
- Scene 4 (0:44–1:00): Critical — the UI must show NO red indicators, NO score, NO negative signal anywhere in frame. If the UI has any red elements in this state, crop or reframe.
- Scene 5 (1:00–1:12): Slow pan across format tiles. Let the viewer read each one. Don't rush.
- Scene 6 (1:12–1:30): Heatmap zoom should feel cinematic — 3–4 second hold before fading to logo. Logo hold for ~3 seconds. Music resolves here.

**Transition style:** Dissolves throughout — no hard cuts. This is cinematic, not promotional.

**Music:** Source from a royalty-free piano/ambient library (e.g. Pixabay, Free Music Archive). Look for "reflective piano" or "hopeful ambient". Keep under the narration at -18dB minimum.

**TTS note:** Feed "Full narration (clean read)" directly to kokoro. Voice suggestion: warm female voice, measured pace — aim for ~115 words/minute. Brief pauses between paragraphs (~0.5s) to let music breathe.

**Logo asset:** If no QuestLearn logo exists yet, use the product name in clean sans-serif on white. Kit can generate this if needed.
