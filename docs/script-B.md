## Full narration (clean read)

Meet QuestLearn — the AI study tool that actually works the way students' brains do. Here's how it works in sixty seconds.

A student opens QuestLearn, types in the topic they're stuck on — say, photosynthesis — and here's where it gets interesting: they choose how they want to learn it.

Five formats to choose from: Game, Story, Meme, Puzzle, or Short Film. The student always chooses — they're never assigned a format. Today they go with Story.

Instantly, QuestLearn generates a curriculum-aligned story — built to Australian Curriculum v9 standards. You can see the curriculum alignment tag right here. The AI then asks a question — not to quiz them, but to guide their thinking.

Here you can see the student answered — and the AI never just tells them the answer. It asks another question to deepen their understanding. Wrong answers don't lose points — there are no points. Personal progress only.

And the teacher sees all of this in real time. This heatmap shows exactly which topics and formats the whole class is engaging with — no individual tracking, just the patterns that matter.

Whether a student's in Bourke or Bondi, they get the same quality AI tutor. That's QuestLearn — learning on your terms.

## Notes for Finn

**Pacing guidance:**
- Scene 1 (0:00–0:10): Fast, punchy opener. Get to the UI immediately after the hook line. No long hold — this is a walkthrough, not cinematic.
- Scene 2 (0:10–0:22): Slow enough for the viewer to read the topic field. Don't rush past the format selector appearing — that's the first "wow, interesting" moment.
- Scene 3 (0:22–0:35): Let the cursor hover across each format tile long enough to read the names. Aim for ~1 second per tile before clicking Story. Viewers should feel the choice.
- Scene 4 (0:35–0:50): The 🧪 Demo stub badge and curriculum tag need to be legible. If they're small in the full card view, consider a quick crop-zoom in after the full-card shot. The word "Story" in the content itself should be visible in the card.
- Scene 5 (0:50–1:05): Key message: NO fail state, NO points, NO competitive mechanics. Confirm this with Anusha before capture. If the chat UI has any score indicators, they need to be absent from this view or flagged to the dev team.
- Scene 6 (1:05–1:22): This is the WOW moment for this audience. Give the heatmap 8–10 seconds of screen time. The hover interaction showing engagement data should be visible and readable. If the heatmap labels are small, zoom in on the most colourful cluster.
- Scene 7 (1:22–1:30): Short and warm. The "Bourke or Bondi" line lands the equity message without the gravity of Variant A — it's a closing promise, not the opening wound. The Bourke/Bondi graphic can be a simple two-panel card Kit generates if there's no app screen for this.

**Transition style:** Clean cuts with brief wipe or slide transitions — slightly more dynamic than Variant A. This is a walkthrough, so each cut should feel like "and now let me show you this."

**Music:** Same library as Variant A but choose a brighter, higher-energy track. Think "product demo" energy — not aggressive, but forward-moving. Keep at -18dB under narration.

**TTS note:** Feed "Full narration (clean read)" directly to kokoro. Voice suggestion: friendly female or neutral voice, slightly faster pace than Variant A — aim for ~120 words/minute. Natural pauses at paragraph breaks but maintain energy throughout.

**Second-person style note:** The narration references "you can see" — make sure the screen state at that moment is exactly what the narration describes. Sync is critical for this walkthrough style. If in doubt, re-record narration to match what's on screen rather than adjusting screenshots.

**Equity note:** Unlike Variant A (which OPENS with the equity problem), Variant B CLOSES with equity as a positive promise. This is intentional — the audience shift means we want the wow-factor first (the product), equity last (the mission). Do not swap the ordering.
