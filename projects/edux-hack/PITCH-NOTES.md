# QuestLearn — Pitch Speaker Notes
## EduX Hackathon 2026 | Challenge 2 — Cogniti Track
### 3-Minute Pitch

---

## Slide 1 — Hook *(~20 seconds)*

> "Here's a question every Australian secondary teacher has asked at some point: why is my student completely switched off in class, but utterly absorbed the moment they get home and open a game or a YouTube video?

> The answer isn't attention span. It's relevance. Students don't disengage because learning is hard — they disengage because the content doesn't speak their language. And teachers know this. But personalising learning for 30 students across 6 classes? At scale? That's not a pedagogical problem. That's an infrastructure problem."

---

## Slide 2 — Solution: QuestLearn + Cogniti *(~40 seconds)*

> "QuestLearn is our answer to that infrastructure problem.

> At its core is CurricuLLM-AU — a content generation engine that takes any Australian Curriculum v9 topic and renders it in five student-chosen formats: a game, a story, a meme, a puzzle, or a short film. Every format is curriculum-aligned. A student who learns better through narrative gets the story. A student who's competitive gets the game. Same content descriptor — different door in.

> But content alone isn't enough. Students need to *do* something with it — recall it, question it, connect it. That's where Cogniti comes in.

> We built QuestLearn as an active layer on top of Cogniti's platform. Every piece of generated content connects directly to a Cogniti experience designed to deepen understanding."

---

## Slide 3 — Cogniti Demo Walk *(~60 seconds)*

> "Let me show you the three Cogniti experiences we integrated.

> **First: the Socratic Tutor.** When a student lands on their learning content, they can open a Cogniti-powered AI tutor alongside it. But this tutor doesn't give answers — it asks questions. Guided Socratic dialogue. The student has to *think*, not copy. They can run it side-by-side with our CurricuLLM-AU tutor and compare the dialogue. [Demo: show the side-by-side view]

> **Second: Flashcards.** After engaging with content, students move to a Cogniti flashcard set. They flip a card, type their answer, rate their own confidence — one, two, or three — and get immediate AI critique on whether their answer actually captured the concept. This is retrieval practice with a feedback loop. [Demo: flip a card, rate, show AI response]

> **Third: Concept Maps.** Students build a visual map of how ideas connect — Photosynthesis links to Light Energy, which links to Chlorophyll, which links to Glucose. Then Cogniti's AI evaluates the map: are the connections accurate? What's missing? This is the highest-order task — synthesis, not just recall. [Demo: show a completed map with AI feedback]

> Together, these three experiences are the *active learning layer* of QuestLearn. Content gets you through the door. Cogniti makes sure something sticks."

---

## Slide 4 — Teacher Story *(~30 seconds)*

> "Now let's talk about the teacher.

> Ms Chen opens the QuestLearn dashboard on a Monday morning. She can see her Year 9 class is strong on Photosynthesis in game format — high engagement, high completion. But story format is dropping off. And flashcard confidence scores for Newton's Laws? Consistently low across the cohort.

> She doesn't need to chase students down for feedback. The data tells her where the class needs her most — and it tells her *how* they prefer to learn it. So she adjusts Monday's lesson. More scaffolding on Newton's Laws. A different format for the story-averse group.

> Data-driven teaching. Without surveillance."

---

## Slide 5 — Why Cogniti *(~20 seconds)*

> "A lot of hackathon projects bolt an AI tool on at the end as an afterthought. We did the opposite.

> QuestLearn was designed *from the ground up* as a layer built on top of Cogniti. The content engine exists to feed Cogniti experiences. The teacher dashboard exists to surface Cogniti telemetry. The whole product is a demonstration of what a fully-realised Cogniti integration for Australian curriculum could look like.

> This is a reference implementation — not a prototype."

---

## Slide 6 — Close *(~10 seconds)*

> "QuestLearn isn't just a hackathon project.

> It's a blueprint for what Cogniti can be for four million Australian secondary students.

> Thank you."

## If You Get a Q&A Question: "How is this different from just using Cogniti directly?"

> "Cogniti is the active learning engine. QuestLearn is the curriculum layer that feeds it. Without CurricuLLM-AU, Cogniti doesn't know what Australian Year 9 Science looks like. Without Cogniti, our content has no active learning component. They're genuinely complementary — neither works as well alone."
